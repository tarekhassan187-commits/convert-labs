1. Why temperature conversion matters more than most people think

Temperature underpins cooking, material selection, HVAC control, medical dosing, scientific experiments and climate data. 4. Practical precision rules

  • Weather & cooking: 0‚Äì1 decimal place is usually fine.
  • Laboratory & engineering: keep at least two or three decimal places until final rounding; follow instrument uncertainty.
  • Sensors & telemetry: store raw readings (floats), format only for display. Round at the UI level, not in the logging pipeline.

5. Sensor & measurement tips (real-world advice)

  • Always calibrate critical thermometers. Many consumer probes drift after months of service.
  • Allow the probe to reach thermal equilibrium with the medium (air, water) before recording ‚Äî fast sampling while moving the probe will mislead.
  • Consider response time (time constant) for the probe ‚Äî thin thermocouples respond faster than large bimetal thermometers.
  • When converting sensor readings in software, apply calibration offsets and scale factors before unit conversion.

6. Common pitfalls & how to avoid them

  • Don't mix units mid-formula ‚Äî convert everything to one scale first (commonly Celsius internally, Kelvin for thermodynamic calcs).
  • Avoid rounding halfway through calculations; it introduces systematic bias for sums or chained conversions.
  • Watch locale differences: some locales use comma as decimal separator. Parse and format numbers explicitly in code.
  • Remember Kelvin cannot be negative ‚Äî validate sensor ranges before converting.
