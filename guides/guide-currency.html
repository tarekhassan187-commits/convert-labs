1. The simple conversion formula

If you have a base currency A and a quoted currency B with a direct rate R (expressed as B per A), the conversion is:

4. Rounding & precision rules

  • Keep calculations at full floating precision; round only for display.
  • Display 2 decimal places for most currencies; use zero for JPY or as local convention dictates.
  • When chaining conversions (A‚ÜíX‚ÜíB), avoid rounding at each step to prevent drift ‚Äî round the final result only.

5. Real-world examples

Travel: If you need 200 EUR and the vendor quotes 1 EUR = 1.08 USD (but charges 1% fee), compute the USD required including fee rather than trusting the sticker rate.

Small business: For invoicing in a foreign currency, lock the mid-market rate on the invoice date, and record the bank/provider fees separately for accounting.

7. Best practices & provider checklist

  • Compare mid-market rate vs provider effective rate (include all fees).
  • Use reputable providers for large transfers (banks, FX platforms with clear fees).
  • For recurring transfers, consider forward contracts or multi-payment plans to reduce volatility risk.
  • Always log exchange details (date, rate, fees) for transparency and audits.
