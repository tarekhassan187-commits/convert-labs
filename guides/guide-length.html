Why unit choice matters

Choosing the right unit ensures your measurements are meaningful and practical. Use millimetres or centimetres for small, precise tasks (electronics, carpentry), metres for rooms and general construction, and kilometres/miles for larger distances. Picking the appropriate unit prevents unnecessary rounding errors and simplifies communication.

Key unit relationships (exact)

  • 1 metre (m) = 100 centimetres (cm)
  • 1 inch (in) = 2.54 cm (exact by definition)
  • 1 foot (ft) = 12 in = 0.3048 m (exact)
  • 1 mile = 1609.344 m

Basic formulas (quick reference)

cm = m * 100
m = cm / 100
in = cm / 2.54
cm = in * 2.54
ft = in / 12
miles = m / 1609.344

  • For repeatable results (baking, parts), use mass rather than volume or length-based proxies.
  • When measuring long distances, use a tape measure or laser rangefinder and record decimals consistently (e.g., 2.34 m not 2 m 34 cm).
