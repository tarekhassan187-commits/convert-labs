Why understanding units matters

Choosing the correct unit (millimetres, centimetres, metres, inches, feet, miles) ensures your measurement is meaningful for the task. Use millimetres for fine engineering, centimetres or inches for everyday measurements, metres for room dimensions and kilometres/miles for distance and travel.

Key unit relationships (exact)

  • 1 inch (in) = 2.54 cm (exact by definition)

Choosing precision and rounding

Common pitfalls & how to avoid them

  • Mixing units: Always verify both source and target units (e.g., US vs UK gallon confusion for volume; for length ensure cm vs mm).
  • Rounding too early: Keep full precision during calculations and round for display only.
